History – Pistachio Origins

Pistachio trees have grown in the Middle East for thousands of years. Pistachios have always been a desired delicacy in this region. Pistachios are mentioned in the Old Testament (Genesis 43:11).

In Persia (modern day Iran), pistachio trade and ownership of pistachio groves meant riches and high status. Legend has it that pistachios were a favorite of the Queen of Sheba, who demanded all her land’s production for herself and her court. Through the conquests of Alexander the Great (334–323 BC), the nut reached Greece. Later, under the rule of the Roman Emperor Tiberius (First Century AD), the nut was also introduced into Italy and Spain.

The Historical Development of American Pistachios

During the 1880s, imported pistachios were popular in the USA, especially with Middle Eastern immigrants. The pistachio received further distribution through vending machines installed in underground train stations, bars, restaurants and other common locations. “A dozen for a nickel” soon developed into a familiar slogan.

In Search of the Perfect Pistachio for America

It was recognized that California’s Central Valley – due to its fertile soil, hot, dry climate and moderately cold winters – offered the ideal growing conditions for the nut. In 1929, American botanist William E. Whitehouse journeyed to Persia (modern day Iran) to collect pistachios. This pursuit came to a close in 1930, when he returned to the USA with a collection of approximately 20 pounds (10 kilograms) of individually selected nuts.

Of all the nuts Whitehouse collected, only one proved useful… Scientists propagated and strengthened the Kerman by budding it to heartier rootstock varieties.

Pistachio Chicken Wings

Pistachios contain aromatic molecules called pyrazines,
and similar compounds are produced when roasting chicken.
This creates a mutually enhancing effect between the two
at the olfactory level.

Ingredients

Steps

  1. Step 1: Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment or foil.
  2. Step 2: In a food processor, pulse pistachios until finely chopped (not powder). Mix with breadcrumbs, Parmesan, garlic, parsley, salt, and pepper.
  3. Step 3: Dip each chicken wing into beaten eggs. Roll in the pistachio mixture, pressing to adhere.
  4. Step 4: Arrange wings on the prepared sheet, drizzle lightly with olive oil or melted butter. Bake 35–40 minutes, flipping halfway, until golden and cooked through.
    35:00
  5. Step 5: Sprinkle with extra crushed pistachios and fresh parsley. Serve hot with lemon wedges or a yogurt-herb dip.
Tips: For extra crunch: toast pistachios lightly before chopping.

Pistachio Gelato

Pistachio gelato is Italy’s signature frozen dessert—
rich, silky, and intensely nutty.

Ingredients

Instructions

  1. Step 1: In a small bowl, whisk 1/4 cup of the milk with the cornstarch until smooth. Set aside.
  2. Step 2: Heat the remaining milk with the sugar in a saucepan until it almost boils (tiny bubbles around the edges).
  3. Step 3: Slowly whisk in the cornstarch slurry. Keep cooking over low heat, whisking constantly, for about 3 minutes until slightly thickened.
  4. Step 4: Remove from the heat. Transfer to another container and chill completely in the refrigerator (overnight is best).
  5. Step 5: Once cold, whisk in the pistachio paste and a few drops of citrus juice until smooth.
  6. Step 6: Churn the mixture in an ice cream maker according to the manufacturer’s instructions, until it reaches soft-serve texture.
  7. Step 7: Transfer to a container, freeze until firm, and serve.
Tips: Cornstarch or a small amount of honey/glucose syrup can reduce gelato crystallization.

Pistachio Latte

A creamy blend of espresso and pistachio paste,
inspired by café trends in Seoul and Milan.

Instructions

  1. Step 1: In a small saucepan, whisk together the pistachio paste, honey (or sugar), and 2–3 tablespoons of the milk. Heat gently until smooth and combined.
  2. Step 2: Add the rest of the milk and heat until warm (do not boil). Froth with a milk frother, whisk, or by shaking in a jar.
  3. Step 3: Brew 1 shot of espresso (or use strong coffee).
  4. Step 4: Pour the pistachio-flavored milk over the espresso in a mug. Stir gently.
  5. Step 5: Sprinkle with chopped pistachios or a pinch of cardamom/cinnamon for garnish.
Tips: Thoroughly stir the pistachio paste with a small amount of hot milk or hot water before mixing. This ensures a smoother flavor.

Pistachio Macaron

The pistachio macaron pairs French technique with a Mediterranean soul—
crisp outside, tender inside, and filled with nutty buttercream.

Ingredients (18–20 macarons)

For the shells

For the pistachio buttercream

Instructions

Make the shells

  1. Sift together almond flour and powdered sugar. Discard any large bits.
  2. In a clean bowl, whip egg whites on medium speed until foamy. Gradually add granulated sugar and beat to stiff peaks. Add coloring if desired.
  3. Gently fold almond flour mixture into egg whites until the batter flows like thick lava and forms ribbons.
  4. Transfer batter to a piping bag with round tip. Pipe 1.5 in (3–4 cm) circles onto a parchment-lined baking sheet.
  5. Tap sheet to release air bubbles. Let shells sit 30–60 minutes until tops are dry to the touch.
  6. Bake at 300°F (150°C) for 15–18 minutes. Shells are done when they have “feet” and lift off cleanly. Cool completely.

Make the buttercream

  1. Beat butter until creamy.
  2. Add powdered sugar and mix until smooth.
  3. Add pistachio paste and blend well.
  4. Adjust consistency with 1–2 tsp cream or milk if needed.

Assemble

  1. Pipe pistachio buttercream onto one shell and sandwich with another.
  2. Store in an airtight container in the fridge for 24 hours before serving.
Tips: Let macarons rest overnight for the best texture—the shell absorbs moisture from the filling to achieve chewiness.

Halibut with Pistachio Soup

In times before cream or starch, ground pistachios thickened sauces like a modern roux—
lending fish stews a silky smooth texture and rich aroma.

Ingredients

Pistachio Soup Base

Vegetable Garnish

Pistachio Pesto

Fish

Instructions

Make the Pistachio Soup Base

  1. Heat olive oil in a large saucepan over medium heat. Add onion, thyme, garlic, and pistachios. Sauté about 5 minutes until golden brown.
  2. Add chicken stock and bring to a boil. Reduce heat and simmer about 20 minutes. Season with salt and pepper. Transfer mixture to a blender, blend until smooth, strain through a fine sieve. Set aside.

Prepare the Vegetable Garnish

  1. In a skillet over medium heat, warm olive oil. Add onion, butternut squash, fennel, celery, and carrots. Sauté about 5 minutes. Season with salt and pepper.
  2. Add white wine and cook until reduced. Add chicken stock and cook until vegetables are tender and liquid is mostly absorbed. Set aside.

Make the Pistachio Pesto

  1. In a mortar and pestle, pound basil, garlic, potato, pistachios, and a pinch of salt until roughly crushed.
  2. Gradually add olive oil while continuing to pound until smooth. Stir in lemon juice, adjust seasoning, and set aside.

Cook the Halibut

  1. Season halibut cubes with salt and pepper.
  2. Heat olive oil in a skillet over medium-high heat. Pan-sear halibut about 2 minutes per side, until golden brown. Drain briefly on paper towels.

To Serve

Ladle pistachio soup into shallow bowls. Spoon vegetables into the center, then place 5–6 pieces of halibut on top. Finish with a dollop of pistachio pesto and a drizzle of olive oil.

A fresh, nutty twist on the classic pesto —
the pistachio’s buttery aroma pairs perfectly with lemon and basil.

Ingredients

Instructions

  1. Step 1: Boil pasta in salted water until al dente. Reserve 1/2 cup pasta water before draining.
